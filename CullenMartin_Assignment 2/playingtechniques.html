Playing Techniques

  • Strumming: this is the most “basic” technique aside from just playing one note at a time and is the recommended place to begin when learning how to play. This technique involves playing several strings at once along with a chord in a rhythmic fashion. Strumming patterns can provide more variation in your playing and consist of “down” and “up” strums in different arrangements. This technique is found in essentially every genre of music where a guitar may be used.

  • Fingerstyle: a more advanced technique where strings are individually plucked, creating a more dynamic and often melodic sound. This technique is especially popular within the folk, country, and blues genres.

  • Percussive: this technique includes tapping, string slaps and guitar body knocks which add rhythm and percussion (obviously) into your playing. This technique is used in conjunction with the other two techniques and can be difficult to learn but provides a whole new sound to your performance.
